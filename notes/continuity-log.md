# Continuity Log

## Current opening

The story opens with Jane already inbound to **St. Jude’s Head** in his black modified Kitfox while an active emergency unfolds at Mercy Cove.

Jane is his last name. His given name can remain withheld or undecided for now. Ruth and official correspondence address him as **Mr. Jane**, while narration and most island people use “Jane” as a surname-style label.

He has arrived alone, carrying limited belongings, tools, documents, and medical experience inherited through years with Elias. He has already corresponded with Ruth Calder before arriving; she knows enough to allow him to present himself in person, but not enough to trust him.

The island is in Alaska, United States. Its systems are legally American, but strained by weather, remoteness, limited staffing, local pride, maritime danger, and rescue necessity.

A serious operational situation is already underway when Jane arrives. St. Jude’s Head has compromised air capability, bad weather, and an active rescue/medical problem at Mercy Cove that makes Jane’s presence difficult to refuse.

## Important continuity decisions

- Jane is his surname.
- Jane is sixteen.
- He claims to be older and properly credentialed.
- The story should avoid repeatedly stating his age directly. Let the tension live in documents, faces, assumptions, and legal pressure.
- Jane’s Kitfox is black.
- Jane hates shoes generally. It is not an airplane-specific gimmick.
- St. Jude’s Head has a proud local standard featuring a severe old-style St. Jude figure.
- The SAR group displays the standard often.
- Most local pilots mark their planes with the standard.
- Jane does not mark the Kitfox with it at first, but this is not a conscious refusal on day one. He is simply not thinking about the emblem yet.
- No one gives Jane the emblem. When he eventually adopts it, he gets it himself.

## Chapter One intent

Chapter One should establish:

- The island’s physical reality: rain, wind, cliffs, harbor, airfield, salt, corrosion.
- Jane’s attachment to the black Kitfox.
- Jane’s prior correspondence with Ruth.
- Ruth and Wade as practical local authorities.
- The serious emergency at Mercy Cove that makes Jane useful quickly.
- The tension between Jane’s competence and his paperwork.
- St. Jude’s standard as background civic texture only. There should be no chapter-one dialogue about putting it on Jane’s plane, and Jane should not yet think of the unmarked Kitfox as a statement.
- Jane and Wade’s first real bond: a shared, minimal-discussion decision to land against Ruth’s direct instruction because the situation on the ground changes.
- Jane is forced to help without his full medical kit because Ruth made him leave it behind.
- The SAR operation’s first reluctant use of him without fully accepting him.
